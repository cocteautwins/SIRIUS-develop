Generate rotation matrix from three Euler angles. 

Euler angles $ \alpha, \beta, \gamma $ define the general rotation as three consecutive rotations\+:
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item about $ \hat e_z $ through the angle $ \gamma $ ( $ 0 \le \gamma < 2\pi $)
\item about $ \hat e_y $ through the angle $ \beta $ ( $ 0 \le \beta \le \pi $)
\item about $ \hat e_z $ through the angle $ \alpha $ ( $ 0 \le \gamma < 2\pi $)
\end{DoxyItemize}

The total rotation matrix is defined as a product of three rotation matrices\+: \[ R(\alpha, \beta, \gamma) = \left( \begin{array}{ccc} \cos(\alpha) & -\sin(\alpha) & 0 \\ \sin(\alpha) & \cos(\alpha) & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array} \right) \left( \begin{array}{ccc} \cos(\beta) & 0 & \sin(\beta) \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -\sin(\beta) & 0 & \cos(\beta) \end{array} \right) \left( \begin{array}{ccc} \cos(\gamma) & -\sin(\gamma) & 0 \\ \sin(\gamma) & \cos(\gamma) & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array} \right) = \left( \begin{array}{ccc} \cos(\alpha) \cos(\beta) \cos(\gamma) - \sin(\alpha) \sin(\gamma) & -\sin(\alpha) \cos(\gamma) - \cos(\alpha) \cos(\beta) \sin(\gamma) & \cos(\alpha) \sin(\beta) \\ \sin(\alpha) \cos(\beta) \cos(\gamma) + \cos(\alpha) \sin(\gamma) & \cos(\alpha) \cos(\gamma) - \sin(\alpha) \cos(\beta) \sin(\gamma) & \sin(\alpha) \sin(\beta) \\ -\sin(\beta) \cos(\gamma) & \sin(\beta) \sin(\gamma) & \cos(\beta) \end{array} \right) \]

Symmetrize scalar function. 

The following operation is performed\+: \[ f({\bf x}) = \frac{1}{N_{sym}} \sum_{{\bf \hat P}} f({\bf \hat P x}) \] For the function expanded in plane-\/waves we have\+: \[ f({\bf x}) = \frac{1}{N_{sym}} \sum_{{\bf \hat P}} \sum_{\bf G} e^{i{\bf G \hat P x}} f({\bf G}) = \frac{1}{N_{sym}} \sum_{{\bf \hat P}} \sum_{\bf G} e^{i{\bf G (Rx + t)}} f({\bf G}) = \frac{1}{N_{sym}} \sum_{{\bf \hat P}} \sum_{\bf G} e^{i{\bf G t}} e^{i{\bf G Rx}} f({\bf G}) \] Now we do a mapping $ {\bf GR} \rightarrow \tilde {\bf G} $ and find expansion coefficients of the symmetry transformed function\+: \[ f(\tilde{\bf G}) = e^{i{\bf G t}} f({\bf G}) \]
